About Me

My name is Dylan Woodrow-Thorne and ever since i was little i have wanted to work within the Gaming industry, more specifically
a game designer. In order to achieve this goal i have made various desicions throughout my academic years to put me further
towards this career.

Personal Statement:

When trying to decide on which A-levels I wanted to study, I really struggled as I had no idea what I wanted to do with my life,
yet there was always a part of me that wanted to work in the gaming industry. This is what lead to me enrolling in my current
college course where I study creative media and gaming, which then revealed to me my dream job, a game designer. This is
why I want to enrol into to university to study game design so that I may one day see my name in the credits of a game.
Gaming has always been one of my passions ever since I turned on my brotherâ€™s Nintendo GameCube when I was 10.But
more than just enjoying the act of playing the games I always wondered why they were fun, how they worked and why certain
games were better than others. This then led me to playing and analysing more and more games until I had un understanding
of the sheer scale of the industry and how games can come in an almost endless amount of different styles or genres. From
story driven games that offer player choice but lack certain gameplay elements, to simple puzzle games that have only one or
two objectives for the player, yet they get played for hours and hours due to their replay value. With this knowledge I feel that I
will be a perfect addition to the industry, as I can also bring to the table a large number of different plans and ideas that will
work. The only thing I am lacking is the knowledge on how to actually work in this industry, hence why I wish to study game
design at university. At college I have learned and practised how to compose a GDD (Game Design Document) which is a
large document that contains all of the information and details about a game that you plan to make. This is a necessary skill
for a game designer, as they need to be able to present their ideas to the whole development team in a clear way. At college,
I have also had to study and describe a large number of different factors to the gaming industry, including defining the industry
as a whole.
